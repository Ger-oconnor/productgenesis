Maturity Framework

Where does your function stand?

Four stages of AI maturity for marketing leaders. The shift from keyword-ranked discovery to AI-cited authority is a structural change — not an update to existing tactics.

1
Aware

Your team uses AI tools for content creation, campaign management, copy drafting, and performance analysis. Efficiency is up. But your marketing strategy is still built for the previous era: SEO-optimised content, keyword-first content planning, and a buyer journey that starts with search and ends with a form fill. You haven't yet measured your brand's AI search presence.

Tell: "We use AI to create faster. Our strategy and measurement framework are unchanged."

2
Piloting

You've started tracking AI search visibility and know roughly where your brand stands in ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini responses. You've tested AEO content formats and understand that the buyers arriving from AI search are fewer in number but further along in intent. You're running experiments but haven't yet restructured your content strategy or creator relationships around AI discoverability.

Tell: "We know our AI search presence and we're testing AEO content. Still building the strategy."

3
Embedding

Your marketing stack connects data to AI assistants via MCP — retail media data, campaign performance, audience signals flow directly into the tools your team uses. You have a Creator AEO programme: systematic partnerships with creators whose content AI systems are trained on and cite. AI search referrals are tracked as a separate attribution source with its own conversion funnel. AI-driven personalisation is standard, not experimental.

Tell: "Creator AEO is a programme, not an experiment. Our stack is MCP-connected."

4
Leading

First-party data connectivity is your primary strategic asset — the infrastructure that enables AI-driven campaigns to outperform at a level competitors with the same model access can't match. Agentic campaign execution is standard: audience discovery, creative testing, and budget reallocation run autonomously within defined parameters. Your marketing team's role has shifted from operating tools to governing systems and setting strategic parameters that agents execute against.

Tell: "First-party data is our moat. Agentic execution is standard. We govern, AI executes."

Frontier Signals

Where best practice is moving

Synthesised from the most recent and impactful content in the Marketing category.

AI-referred traffic converts at 4.4x the rate of organic search. Your attribution model is almost certainly undercounting it.

Conductor tracked AI-referred sessions across 13,000+ enterprise domains: 527% growth year over year, converting at 4.4x the rate of traditional organic search. HubSpot State of Marketing 2026 corroborates: 58% of marketers report AI-referred visitors convert at higher rates. The mechanism: buyers arriving through AI have already researched, received an answer citing your brand, and click through with specific intent. Attribution models built around click-through rate and keyword ranking are calibrated for a traffic pattern that's no longer dominant — and will show AI search as underperforming when it's actually your best-converting channel.

Source: Conductor
27% of marketers haven't changed their AI search strategy. 16% don't know what GEO means. The gap will show up in attribution before it shows up in revenue.

Digiday surveyed 142 brand and agency professionals: 34% are adapting content for AI answers, 27% haven't changed anything, 16% have never heard of GEO or AEO. That 16% manages significant media budgets. When AI Mode has 1 billion monthly users with queries doubling quarterly, the gap between teams with AI search visibility and those without shows up in attribution data before it shows up in revenue. The recommended starting point: restructure existing content so AI crawlers can identify the authoritative answers within it — don't build from scratch.

Source: Digiday
Marketing has crossed from AI tool to AI infrastructure. 87% use it. 93% budget for it. The org structure to govern it hasn't been built.

When AI is infrastructure rather than a tool, the marketer's job shifts from operating the tool to setting parameters, reviewing outputs, and governing the system's behaviour. That requires different skills, oversight mechanisms, and a different relationship between marketing leadership and the AI layer. Teams in the tool mindset ask "which AI tool should we use?" Teams in the infrastructure mindset ask "what governance do we need for the AI systems running our campaigns?" — and they're building measurement frameworks and quality standards for AI-generated output. The gap between these two groups is widening in output quality, measurement accuracy, and CFO conversation credibility.

Source: Michael MacKenzie
OpenAI activated cost-per-action ads in ChatGPT. Performance marketing just got a new channel — and an attribution model to build around it.

OpenAI's CPA ads went live on May 28, completing a pricing architecture that began with $60 CPM at launch in February. By late May, CPMs have slid to roughly $25; CPCs run $3–5. CPA means marketers pay only when a user clicks, signs up, or purchases — unlocking ChatGPT as a measurable performance channel, not just a brand awareness one. The OpenAI conversion pixel is required. For marketing teams, the immediate action items are: set up conversion tracking before running CPA campaigns, build a separate attribution funnel for ChatGPT-referred conversions (these buyers arrive with different intent than organic or paid search), and establish CPM and CPC benchmarks now while inventory is relatively inexpensive.

Source: Digiday
Priority Actions

Your next move

Locate your maturity stage above, then focus on the actions that move you to the next one.

Stage 1 → 2  ·  Aware to Piloting
  • Measure your brand's AI search presence today. Ask ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini about your product category. Document what they say — and what they say about competitors. Use AIEthos for systematic tracking.
  • Redesign 10–15% of your content for AEO: specific, authoritative, structured to be cited in an AI-generated answer rather than to rank for a keyword. Track the difference in AI referral traffic and engagement quality.
  • Map where your buyers are now doing their research. How many have already done multi-turn AI research before their first interaction with your sales team?
Stage 2 → 3  ·  Piloting to Embedding
  • Launch a Creator AEO programme. Identify 5–10 creator partnerships — journalists, analysts, YouTubers, podcast hosts — whose content AI systems are trained on and cite in your category. Build the relationships now, before this becomes a standard marketing investment.
  • Connect your marketing data stack to AI assistants via MCP. Start with one data source (retail media, campaign performance) and eliminate one dashboard your team currently switches to manually.
  • Add AI search referrals as a separate attribution source in your analytics. Build a distinct conversion funnel for these buyers — they need different content and different CTAs than top-of-funnel organic traffic.
Stage 3 → 4  ·  Embedding to Leading
  • Map your first-party data assets and treat them as competitive infrastructure. The Publicis/LiveRamp acquisition thesis — that data connectivity is the moat in agentic marketing — is the strategic frame to build toward.
  • Implement agentic campaign execution for one channel: audience discovery, creative testing, and budget reallocation running autonomously within defined parameters. Measure performance against human-managed equivalent.
  • Redefine your marketing team's role: from operating tools to governing systems. Which decisions stay with humans? Which parameters do agents operate within? That governance design is the work.
