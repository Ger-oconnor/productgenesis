Strategy & Competitive Positioning

For: CEO · CPO · Chief Strategy Officer · General Manager

AI is commoditising execution advantages faster than most boards realise. The moats that matter now are different from the ones you've spent years building — and the window to build new ones is closing.

Maturity Framework

Where does your function stand?

Four stages of AI strategy maturity. The question isn't whether you're using AI — it's whether the advantage you're building compounds over time.

1
Aware

You track AI competitor moves and have an AI strategy section in your board deck. Your team uses AI tools and you've approved internal experiments. But you haven't yet audited which of your existing competitive advantages are defensible against AI pressure — and which are quietly eroding.

Tell: "We have an AI strategy. We're watching what competitors do."

2
Piloting

You've identified 2–3 AI use cases and are building business cases for them. You understand that some existing moats are under pressure but haven't yet mapped which ones specifically. You're watching whether to build or buy, which model to use, which use case to prioritise — but haven't committed to a structural answer.

Tell: "We're building business cases and watching which bets hold up."

3
Embedding

You've audited your existing moats against AI pressure and identified which are eroding (seat-based licenses, workflow automation built on human labour) and which are holding (network effects, proprietary data). You're actively building one of the three durable AI advantages. Your AI investments have a clear "why this compounds" logic, not just a "why this is efficient" logic.

Tell: "We know which moats hold and we're compounding one of them."

4
Leading

Your AI advantage is one of the three durable types: proprietary data that improves with use, organisational velocity where AI is embedded in decision-making workflows, or physical-world integration that software alone can't replicate. You've designed your operating model for autonomous execution — humans set direction, AI executes — and built the governance infrastructure that makes that trustworthy. You have a genuine, defensible point of view your product stands for that AI can't generate.

Tell: "Our AI advantage compounds. Competitors can't replicate it by buying a better model."

Frontier Signals

Where best practice is moving

Synthesised from the most recent and impactful content in the Strategy category.

Enterprise AI is no longer piloting. KPMG: 276,000 people. EY: $1B over five years.

The largest professional services firms have stopped evaluating AI integration and committed to it as operating infrastructure. PwC, KPMG, EY, and Deloitte have all made firm-wide strategic commitments in the past 60 days. For enterprise product teams selling to these firms, the baseline expectation for AI integration is rising with every partnership. The "exploring AI" positioning that was credible in 2024 is no longer credible in this environment.

Source: Anthropic / KPMG  ·  Microsoft News / EY
Three types of AI moats actually compound. Most strategies are building none of them.

McKinsey's analysis identifies the three mechanisms through which AI advantage compounds: Amazon's proprietary data flywheel (every transaction generates signal competitors can't access), DBS Bank's organisational velocity (AI embedded so deeply in decision workflows that competitive speed is structural, not technical), and John Deere's physical-world integration (training data that requires an equipment fleet to collect). When a competitor accesses the same foundation model next month, these three things are what your product still has.

Source: McKinsey & Company
SAP named the next era: the Autonomous Enterprise. Humans govern. AI executes.

SAP — with 300 million users across every major industry — restructured its keynote around a precise operating model: AI agents execute core business workflows while human teams set strategic direction and govern outcomes. This isn't a vision statement; it's a product architecture decision. Teams still designing for AI-assisted workflows are designing for a model their platform vendors have already moved past. The governance layer — approval workflows, rollback capabilities, audit trails — is now as important as the execution layer.

Source: SAP · YouTube
Anthropic is closing a $30B round at $900B+ valuation — surpassing OpenAI as the world's most valuable private AI company.

Two $30B rounds in 14 weeks, compressing valuation from $380B to $900B+ as the market prices in Anthropic's enterprise trajectory. For enterprise product teams choosing which AI platform to build on, valuation is a proxy for platform stability. The competitive frame of "OpenAI for consumer, Anthropic for enterprise" is now being priced with precision. The platform stability bet the round makes explicit: Claude's enterprise position is durable, and the organisations making seven- and eight-figure commitments to it are making the right call.

Source: TechTimes
Priority Actions

Your next move

Locate your maturity stage above, then focus on the actions that move you to the next one.

Stage 1 → 2  ·  Aware to Piloting
  • Answer one question honestly: when a competitor has the same model access next month, what does your product still have that they don't? If the answer is "nothing yet," that's your strategic priority.
  • Run the Morningstar moat audit on your business: which of your competitive advantages are built on human labour and workflow clicks (collapsing under AI), versus network effects and proprietary data (holding)?
  • Stop treating AI strategy and product strategy as separate conversations. Schedule one combined session.
Stage 2 → 3  ·  Piloting to Embedding
  • Pick one durable advantage to compound. Data flywheel, org velocity, or physical-world integration — and start generating that asset deliberately, not as a by-product.
  • Define your product's genuine point of view. Not a positioning statement — a specific intellectual position on how the problem should be solved that AI can't generate and competitors can't copy in a sprint.
  • Map your existing enterprise relationships. If your buyers are inside KPMG, EY, or PwC ecosystems, the AI model they've committed to is now a decision factor for your integration story.
Stage 3 → 4  ·  Embedding to Leading
  • Design your operating model for the Autonomous Enterprise framing: humans set direction, AI executes. Which of your core business workflows can be redesigned for agent execution with human governance?
  • Build the governance infrastructure alongside the execution layer. Approval workflows, rollback capabilities, audit trails. Autonomous execution without governance is a compliance risk, not a productivity win.
  • Assess whether your AI advantage is replicable. If a well-resourced competitor can match it by buying a better model in Q3, it's not a moat — it's table stakes.
