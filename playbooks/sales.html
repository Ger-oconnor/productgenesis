Maturity Framework

Where does your function stand?

Four stages of AI maturity for sales leaders. The distinction that matters is between AI that makes individual sellers faster and AI that changes your team's competitive position.

1
Aware

Your reps use AI tools for drafting outreach, summarising calls, researching accounts, and updating CRM records. Individual productivity has improved. But the sales process itself — discovery, qualification, objection handling, deal progression — follows the same motion as before. AI is making individuals faster at the existing workflow, not changing the workflow.

Tell: "Our reps use AI tools and save time. Process and targets haven't changed."

2
Piloting

You're running AI SDR experiments or hybrid outreach models. AI handles some prospecting volume. You're evaluating which parts of the top-of-funnel can be handled autonomously and which require human judgment. You've started asking what reps do with AI-freed time — but haven't yet given them an explicit answer or redesigned their role around it.

Tell: "We're running AI SDR tests and hybrid outreach. Still figuring out the right model."

3
Embedding

AI is workflow-level: researching accounts, qualifying leads, updating CRM, routing deals, and running outreach plays autonomously. Sellers have explicit reinvestment targets for AI-freed time — redirected to discovery quality, multi-threaded relationship building, and enterprise navigation. The discovery call's purpose has been redefined: it's validation and context-adding, not information delivery. You measure seller activity quality, not just volume.

Tell: "AI runs the workflow. Reps focus on validation, relationship depth, and complex deals."

4
Leading

AI handles the full top-of-funnel at scale: processing 1,000+ contacts daily, adapting messaging based on engagement signals, escalating to a human at the right threshold. Sellers are expert validators and relationship depth builders — the trust layer in a world where buyers arrive having done AI research. Your AI system compounds every quarter through closed-loop learning: every interaction updates the next prospecting decision. CRM, conversation intelligence, and forecasting are connected.

Tell: "AI runs top-of-funnel. Reps are expert validators. The system compounds quarter over quarter."

87% of sales teams use AI for tasks: prospecting, forecasting, email drafting. 54% use agents for automation. Results are inconsistent because automating the existing task doesn't change the decision architecture that determines whether the right work is happening. Teams that start from decisions change outcomes. Teams that start from tools automate the visible work.

Priority Actions

Your next move

Locate your maturity stage above, then focus on the actions that move you to the next one.

Stage 1 → 2  ·  Aware to Piloting
  • Audit your reps' AI time savings. Ask each rep where the saved time is going. If the answer is "more of the same tasks," you have a reinvestment problem — not an adoption problem.
  • Redesign the discovery call's stated purpose. Brief your team: buyers now arrive having done AI research. The meeting is validation and context-adding, not information delivery. What does that call look like?
  • Run a controlled test: one group using AI for outreach and prospecting, one not. Measure response rates, conversion rates, and time-to-first-meeting separately.
Stage 2 → 3  ·  Piloting to Embedding
  • Set explicit reinvestment targets for AI-freed time. Define what 3–4 of those hours per seller per week should go toward: deeper account research, multi-threaded champion building, enterprise navigation. Make it measurable.
  • Redesign at least one AI SDR deployment to match the 2% that works: precise deployment at the top of funnel for a specific ACV range, with a clear human handoff trigger at the first positive reply.
  • Connect your AI outreach tools to your CRM. Closed-loop learning — where every interaction updates the next prospecting decision — requires the connection. Without it, AI improves volume but doesn't compound.
Stage 3 → 4  ·  Embedding to Leading
  • Evaluate autonomous CRM: Clarify has opened its Agents feature to startups, and Salesforce Agentforce is available at enterprise. Assess whether your CRM is a system of record (where humans update it) or a system of action (where agents research, draft, and update autonomously).
  • Build a signal quality framework for your AI prospecting. Volume is not the win — understanding buyer readiness signals (internal movement, trigger events, urgency indicators) is what separates the 15% AI SDR conversion rate from the 25%+ human rate.
  • Connect all four data layers: CRM, conversation intelligence, forecast, and AI outreach. The compound effect requires the full loop — and most teams are missing at least one connection.
